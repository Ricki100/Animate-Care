Roles

Roles we recruit for

Residential Support Worker

Provide day-to-day care, routines, key working and therapeutic support for young people.

Senior Support Worker

Guide shifts, support colleagues and help maintain high standards of care and documentation.

Waking Night Support Worker

Provide overnight care, supervision and reassurance throughout the night.

Therapeutic Childcare Worker

Bring trauma-aware practice, emotional regulation support and relational care into daily life.

Bank Support Worker

Offer flexible support while maintaining the same safeguarding and care standards.

What we look for

Values matter as much as experience

We look for people who are calm, boundaried, reflective and genuinely committed to young people. Experience in residential childcare is valuable, but attitude and values are essential.

  • Warmth, patience and emotional resilience.
  • Commitment to safeguarding and professional boundaries.
  • Ability to reflect, learn and accept supervision.
  • Confidence with routines, documentation and team communication.
  • Respect for children's identity, culture and voice.
  • Willingness to complete required training and checks.

Careers: questions answered

What jobs does Animate Care recruit for?

We recruit residential support workers, senior support workers, waking night support workers and therapeutic childcare professionals.

Do I need experience in residential care to apply?

Experience is valuable but not always essential. Values, attitude and commitment to young people matter deeply.

Does Animate Care have waking night positions?

Yes. Waking night support workers provide overnight care and supervision.

What checks are required?

Safer recruitment includes enhanced DBS, full employment history review, references and induction.
